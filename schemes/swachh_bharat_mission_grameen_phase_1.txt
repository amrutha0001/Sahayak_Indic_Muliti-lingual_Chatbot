Swachh Bharat Mission – Grameen (Phase I)

Ministry: Ministry of Jal Shakti  
Department: Department of Drinking Water and Sanitation  
Launch Date: 2 October 2014  
Type: Centrally Sponsored Rural Sanitation Scheme  

--------------------------------------------------

1. Overview:

Swachh Bharat Mission – Grameen (Phase I) was launched in 2014 with the objective of eliminating open defecation in rural India through behavioral change and construction of household and community toilets.

The scheme focused on providing financial assistance and awareness interventions to poor, underprivileged, and marginalized rural households.

Objectives:

- To bring about large-scale behavioral change regarding sanitation and hygiene.
- To ensure construction and sustained usage of household and community toilets.
- To establish an accountable mechanism for achieving Open Defecation Free (ODF) status in India.
- To promote Solid and Liquid Waste Management (SLWM).

--------------------------------------------------

2. Benefits:

- Financial assistance of Rs. 12,000 per toilet for eligible rural households.
- Improved rural sanitation coverage through construction of individual and community toilets.
- Reduction in open defecation practices across rural India.

Activities under the Scheme:

- Construction of Individual Household Latrines (IHHL).
- Construction of Community Sanitary Complexes (CSCs).
- Solid and Liquid Waste Management (SLWM) activities.
- Information, Education and Communication (IEC) activities.
- Human Resource Development (HRD) initiatives.

--------------------------------------------------

3. Eligibility:

- Applicant must belong to a rural household that did not have a toilet prior to the scheme.
- Applicant should belong to poor, underprivileged, or marginalized sections.

Preference is given to:
- Women-led households
- Elderly persons
- Persons with disabilities
- Socio-economically weaker sections

Exclusions:

- Households already possessing functional toilets were not eligible for financial assistance under the scheme.

--------------------------------------------------

6. Frequently Asked Questions (Selected):

Q: What is the aim of the scheme?
A: To eliminate open defecation in rural areas through toilet construction and behavioral change.

Q: Who is eligible to apply?
A: Rural households without existing toilets, especially poor and marginalized sections.

Q: Are households with existing toilets eligible?
A: No, households with functional toilets are not eligible for financial assistance.

Q: What types of toilets were promoted?
A: Individual household toilets and community sanitary complexes.

Q: What is twin pit toilet technology?
A: Twin pit technology uses two pits alternately, allowing safe decomposition of waste and sustainable sanitation management.

Q: What is the IMIS system?
A: IMIS (Integrated Management Information System) is used for monitoring and tracking implementation under the scheme.
