Ayushman Bharat – Pradhan Mantri Jan Arogya Yojana (PM-JAY)

Ministry: Ministry of Health & Family Welfare  
Department: National Health Authority (NHA)  
Launch Date: 23 September 2018  
Type: National Public Health Insurance Scheme  

--------------------------------------------------

1. Overview:

Ayushman Bharat – Pradhan Mantri Jan Arogya Yojana (PM-JAY) is a national health insurance scheme launched by the Government of India to provide cashless hospitalization coverage to poor and vulnerable families.

The scheme provides health insurance coverage of Rs. 5,00,000 per family per year for secondary and tertiary healthcare services.

Ayushman Bharat adopts a continuum of care approach and consists of two interrelated components:

1. Health and Wellness Centres (HWCs):
   Provide comprehensive primary healthcare services including:
   - Maternal and child health services
   - Non-communicable disease care
   - Free essential drugs
   - Diagnostics
   - Preventive healthcare promotion

2. Pradhan Mantri Jan Arogya Yojana (PM-JAY):
   Provides cashless insurance coverage up to Rs. 5,00,000 per family per year for hospitalization.

--------------------------------------------------

2. Benefits:

Financial Health Insurance Cover:

- Annual coverage of up to Rs. 5,00,000 per family per year.
- Cashless and paperless treatment at empanelled hospitals.
- Coverage includes:
  - Consultation
  - Medicines
  - Diagnostics
  - Surgery
  - ICU charges
  - Implants
  - Accommodation and food
- Covers 3 days pre-hospitalization expenses.
- Covers 15 days post-hospitalization expenses.
- Covers treatment complications.

Coverage Features:

- Covers all pre-existing diseases from day one.
- No restriction on family size, age, or gender.
- Nationwide portability across empanelled hospitals.
- Covers approximately 1,929 medical procedures.

--------------------------------------------------

3. Eligibility:

Rural Beneficiaries:

Families falling under at least one of the following deprivation criteria:

- Only one room with kutcha walls and roof.
- No adult member between ages 16–59.
- No adult male member between ages 16–59.
- Disabled member with no able-bodied adult.
- SC/ST households.
- Landless households dependent on manual casual labour.

Automatically included:

- Households without shelter.
- Destitute or living on alms.
- Manual scavenger families.
- Primitive tribal groups.
- Legally released bonded labour.

Urban Beneficiaries:

Eligible occupational categories include:

- Ragpickers
- Beggars
- Domestic workers
- Street vendors / hawkers / cobblers
- Construction workers / labourers / plumbers / painters / welders / security guards
- Sanitation workers
- Home-based workers / artisans / tailors
- Transport workers / drivers / conductors / rickshaw pullers
- Shop assistants / helpers / delivery assistants
- Electricians / mechanics / repair workers
- Washer-men / chowkidars

Exclusions:

Not eligible if:

- Own two-, three-, or four-wheeler vehicles or motorized fishing boat.
- Own mechanized farming equipment.
- Have Kisan Credit Card with limit above Rs. 50,000.
- Are government employees.
- Work in government-managed non-agricultural enterprises.
- Earn monthly income above Rs. 10,000.
- Own refrigerator and landline.
- Own solidly built houses.
- Own 5 acres or more agricultural land.

4. Application Process:

Mode: Offline and Online

To obtain PM-JAY e-card:

Step 1:
Visit an empanelled hospital or Community Service Centre (CSC).

Step 2:
Provide identification details such as:
- PM letter
- RSBY URN
- Ration card number
- Mobile number

Step 3:
Arogya Mitra searches beneficiary details in the BIS system.

Step 4:
Individual identification through Aadhaar or government ID and ration card.

Step 5:
Family records are verified and uploaded.

Step 6:
Trust/insurance company reviews and approves or rejects.

Step 7:
On approval, PM-JAY e-card is issued.

--------------------------------------------------

5. Documents Required:

- Aadhaar Card or PAN Card (Identity Proof)
- Proof of Address
- Mobile number / contact details
- Caste Certificate (if applicable)
- Income Certificate
- Family status document (Joint/Nuclear)
- Ration Card

--------------------------------------------------

6. Frequently Asked Questions:

Q: Do beneficiaries need to pay any premium?
A: No. Eligible families receive coverage free of cost.

Q: Are pre-existing diseases covered?
A: Yes. All pre-existing diseases are covered from day one.

Q: Is there any limit on family size?
A: No. There is no cap on family size, age, or gender.

Q: Can benefits be availed without Aadhaar?
A: Aadhaar or valid government ID is required for identification and verification.

Q: Is the scheme portable across India?
A: Yes. Treatment can be availed at any empanelled hospital across India.
