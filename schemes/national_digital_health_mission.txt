National Digital Health Mission (NDHM) / Ayushman Bharat Digital Mission (ABDM)

Ministry: Ministry of Health & Family Welfare  
Department: National Health Authority (NHA)  
Launch Date: 15 August 2020  
Type: National Digital Health Infrastructure Mission  

--------------------------------------------------

1. Overview:

The National Digital Health Mission (NDHM), later renamed Ayushman Bharat Digital Mission (ABDM), aims to create a unified digital health ecosystem in India.

The mission establishes:

• Unique Health IDs (ABHA ID)
• Digital Health Records
• Health Facility Registry
• Healthcare Professionals Registry
• Interoperable digital health platforms

Objective:

• Improve access to healthcare services.
• Enable seamless data sharing with consent.
• Reduce duplication of medical tests.
• Strengthen digital governance in healthcare.

The system is built on consent-based data sharing architecture.

--------------------------------------------------

2. Benefits:

1. ABHA ID:
   • Unique digital health identifier for citizens.

2. Digital Health Records:
   • Secure storage of prescriptions, reports, diagnosis.

3. Interoperability:
   • Records accessible across hospitals with consent.

4. Telemedicine Integration:
   • Enables remote consultation.

5. Reduced Medical Costs:
   • Avoid repeat tests.

6. Nationwide Portability:
   • Records accessible across India.

--------------------------------------------------

3. Eligibility:

Eligible:

• All Indian citizens.
• Healthcare providers.
• Diagnostic labs.
• Pharmacies.

Exclusions:

• No mandatory participation.
• Participation voluntary and consent-based.

--------------------------------------------------

4. Application Process:

For Citizens:

Step 1: Visit ABDM portal.
Step 2: Generate ABHA ID using Aadhaar/mobile.
Step 3: Complete OTP verification.
Step 4: Create login credentials.

For Healthcare Providers:

• Register through ABDM portal.
• Submit registration and compliance documents.

Consent-Based Data Sharing:

• Records shared only after user approval.

--------------------------------------------------

5. Required Documents:

• Aadhaar (optional but recommended)
• Mobile number
• Identity proof (if required)
• Healthcare provider registration certificate (for institutions)

--------------------------------------------------

6. Frequently Asked Questions:

Q1: Is ABHA ID mandatory?
A: No, voluntary.

Q2: Is my data secure?
A: Yes, consent-based architecture.

Q3: Can hospitals access data without permission?
A: No.

Q4: Is it linked to PM-JAY?
A: It complements but is separate.

Q5: Is there any fee?
A: No.
