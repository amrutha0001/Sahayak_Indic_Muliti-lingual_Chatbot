Beti Bachao Beti Padhao (BBBP)

Ministry: Ministry of Women & Child Development  
Department: Department of Women & Child Development  
Launch Date: 22 January 2015  
Type: National Social Reform & Awareness Campaign  

--------------------------------------------------

1. Overview:

Beti Bachao Beti Padhao (BBBP), meaning “Save the Daughter, Educate the Daughter,” is a national initiative aimed at addressing declining Child Sex Ratio (CSR) and promoting the education, survival, and empowerment of the girl child.

The scheme was launched in response to:

• Declining child sex ratio due to gender-biased sex selection  
• Discrimination against girl children  
• Lower female literacy and education levels  
• Social inequality and patriarchal norms  

The scheme operates through a multi-sectoral approach combining:

• Advocacy and awareness campaigns  
• Enforcement of PCPNDT Act (Pre-Conception and Pre-Natal Diagnostic Techniques Act)  
• Strengthening service delivery for girl child welfare  
• Promoting girls’ education and enrollment  
• Community mobilization  

The scheme initially targeted 100 gender-critical districts and was later expanded nationwide.

--------------------------------------------------

2. Benefits:

1. Awareness and Behavioral Change Campaigns:
   • Mass media campaigns.
   • Community engagement programs.
   • School-level sensitization.

2. Strengthening Girl Child Education:
   • Promotion of enrollment and retention of girls in schools.
   • Support for secondary education of girls.

3. Improved Service Delivery:
   • Better monitoring of sex ratio at birth.
   • Strengthened maternal health services.

4. Legal Enforcement:
   • Strict implementation of PCPNDT Act.
   • Monitoring of illegal sex determination practices.

5. Convergence with Other Schemes:
   • Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana.
   • Scholarship programs for girls.
   • Health and nutrition schemes.

--------------------------------------------------

3. Eligibility:

• Universal initiative — applicable nationwide.
• Focus on girl children across all socio-economic backgrounds.
• Special focus on districts with low child sex ratio.

Exclusions:

• No direct cash transfer component.
• Not an individual financial benefit scheme.

--------------------------------------------------

4. Application Process:

Mode: Implemented through government departments.

There is no individual application process.

Implementation Mechanism:

Step 1:
District-level planning and identification of focus areas.

Step 2:
Awareness campaigns conducted via schools, Anganwadis, Panchayats.

Step 3:
Monitoring through district task forces.

Step 4:
Data tracking on child sex ratio and girl education.

--------------------------------------------------

5. Required Documents:

• Not applicable for individuals.
• Administrative records maintained by government authorities.

--------------------------------------------------

6. Frequently Asked Questions:

Q1: Does BBBP provide money directly to families?
A: No, it is primarily an awareness and institutional strengthening scheme.

Q2: Is this linked to Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana?
A: Yes, it promotes awareness of girl-child savings schemes.

Q3: Who implements BBBP?
A: Ministry of Women & Child Development with partner ministries.

Q4: What problem does it address?
A: Declining child sex ratio and gender discrimination.

Q5: Is it active nationwide?
A: Yes.
