Pradhan Mantri Awas Yojana (PMAY)

Ministry: 
- Ministry of Housing & Urban Affairs (PMAY-Urban)  
- Ministry of Rural Development (PMAY-Gramin)  

Launch Date: 25 June 2015  
Type: Central Government Housing Scheme  

----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

1. Overview:

Pradhan Mantri Awas Yojana (PMAY) is a flagship housing initiative of the Government of India aimed at providing “Housing for All.” The scheme seeks to ensure that every eligible family has access to a pucca house with basic civic amenities.

PMAY operates in two components:

• PMAY-Urban (PMAY-U) – For urban beneficiaries  
• PMAY-Gramin (PMAY-G) – For rural beneficiaries  

The scheme provides financial assistance for:

• Construction of new houses  
• Enhancement of existing houses  
• Purchase of affordable housing  
• Interest subsidy on home loans  

Under PMAY-Urban, the Credit Linked Subsidy Scheme (CLSS) provides interest subsidy on housing loans for EWS, LIG, and MIG categories.

PMAY-Gramin focuses on providing financial assistance to rural poor households identified under SECC data.

The scheme promotes:

• Affordable housing
• Women ownership (mandatory co-ownership in many cases)
• Environment-friendly construction
• Convergence with sanitation, electricity, and LPG schemes

--------------------------------------------------

2. Benefits:

1. Financial Assistance (PMAY-G):
   • ₹1.20 lakh in plain areas.
   • ₹1.30 lakh in hilly/difficult areas.

2. Credit Linked Subsidy Scheme (PMAY-U):
   • Interest subsidy up to 6.5% for EWS/LIG.
   • Subsidy applied to loan tenure up to 20 years.
   • Subsidy credited upfront to loan account.

3. House Size:
   • Minimum 25 sq. meter area (including cooking space).

4. Convergence Benefits:
   • Swachh Bharat toilet support.
   • Electricity connection under Saubhagya.
   • LPG under Ujjwala.
   • Drinking water supply schemes.

5. Direct Benefit Transfer:
   • Funds transferred directly to beneficiary bank account in stages.

--------------------------------------------------

3. Eligibility:

General Eligibility:

• Must not own a pucca house anywhere in India.
• Must belong to EWS/LIG/MIG category (Urban).
• Must be listed in SECC data (Rural).

Income Criteria (Urban):

• EWS: Annual income up to ₹3 lakh.
• LIG: ₹3–6 lakh.
• MIG-I: ₹6–12 lakh.
• MIG-II: ₹12–18 lakh.

Special Provisions:

• House ownership preferably in woman’s name.
• Priority to SC/ST, minorities, differently-abled, widows.

Exclusions:

• Those owning pucca house.
• Income tax payers in certain categories.
• High-income category applicants.

--------------------------------------------------

4. Application Process:

Mode: Online and Offline

Online (PMAY-U):

Step 1: Visit official PMAY portal.
Step 2: Select “Citizen Assessment.”
Step 3: Enter Aadhaar number.
Step 4: Fill income and family details.
Step 5: Submit form.
Step 6: Verification by Urban Local Body.

PMAY-G:

• Identified via SECC data.
• Verified by Gram Sabha.
• Approved list published at village level.

Loan-based CLSS:

• Apply through bank/HFC.
• Bank verifies eligibility.
• Subsidy processed through National Housing Bank.

--------------------------------------------------

5. Documents Required:

• Aadhaar Card
• Income certificate
• Bank account details
• Property documents
• SECC inclusion proof (rural)
• Loan sanction letter (CLSS)

--------------------------------------------------

6. Frequently Asked Questions:

Q1: Can I apply if I already own a house?
A: No, existing pucca house owners are not eligible.

Q2: Is subsidy given directly to me?
A: Under CLSS, subsidy is credited to loan account.

Q3: Is women ownership mandatory?
A: Preferred, and mandatory in many categories.

Q4: Can I apply offline?
A: Yes, through ULBs or Gram Panchayats.

Q5: Is there any application fee?
A: No official application fee.
