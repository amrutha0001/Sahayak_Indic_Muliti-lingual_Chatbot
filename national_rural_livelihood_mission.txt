Deendayal Antyodaya Yojana – National Rural Livelihood Mission (DAY-NRLM)

Ministry: Ministry of Rural Development  
Department: Department of Rural Development  
Launch Date: 3 June 2011 (restructured as DAY-NRLM in 2015)  
Type: Centrally Sponsored Scheme  

--------------------------------------------------

1. Overview:

DAY-NRLM aims to reduce poverty by organizing rural poor households, especially women, into Self Help Groups (SHGs) and providing them sustainable livelihood opportunities.

The mission follows a community-driven approach and focuses on:

• Financial inclusion
• Livelihood diversification
• Skill development
• Institutional strengthening

Core Strategy:

• Universal social mobilization
• Formation of SHGs and federations
• Bank linkage and credit access
• Capacity building
• Promotion of farm and non-farm livelihoods

Funding Pattern:

• 60:40 (Centre:State)
• 90:10 for NE & Himalayan States

--------------------------------------------------

2. Benefits:

1. Self Help Group Formation:
   - 10–20 women per SHG.
   - Savings-led model.

2. Revolving Fund:
   - ₹10,000–₹15,000 per SHG (approximate).

3. Community Investment Fund (CIF):
   - Financial support routed through SHG federations.

4. Bank Credit Linkage:
   - Priority sector lending.
   - Collateral-free loans.

5. Interest Subvention:
   - Reduction in effective interest rate for timely repayment.

6. Skill Development:
   - Placement-linked skill training.
   - Rural Self Employment Training Institutes (RSETIs).

7. Livelihood Promotion:
   - Agriculture-based interventions.
   - Livestock support.
   - Micro-enterprise support.

--------------------------------------------------

3. Eligibility:

Eligible:

• Rural poor households identified through Participatory Identification of Poor (PIP).
• Priority to women from economically weaker backgrounds.
• SC/ST households.
• Minority communities.
• Persons with disabilities.

Not Eligible:

• Urban households.
• High-income households.
• Institutional beneficiaries.

Special Provisions:

• At least one woman per poor household encouraged to join SHG.
• Focus on marginalized communities.

--------------------------------------------------

4. Application Process:

Step 1:
Village-level mobilization and awareness.

Step 2:
Identification of poor households via PIP process.

Step 3:
Formation of SHG.

Step 4:
Opening SHG bank account.

Step 5:
Internal savings and lending begins.

Step 6:
Revolving fund support provided.

Step 7:
Bank credit linkage facilitated.

Step 8:
Skill training or livelihood support provided.

No direct online application by individuals.
Entry through community mobilization.

--------------------------------------------------

5. Documents Required:

• Aadhaar
• Bank account details
• SHG membership records
• Income certificate (if required)
• Rural residence proof

--------------------------------------------------

6. Frequently Asked Questions:

Q1: Who can form SHG?
A: 10–20 rural poor women in a village.

Q2: Is loan collateral required?
A: No, collateral-free loans provided under priority lending.

Q3: What is interest subvention?
A: Government reduces effective interest burden for timely repayment.

Q4: Can men form SHGs?
A: Primarily women-focused, but other structures may exist.

Q5: Is training free?
A: Many programs are subsidized or free.

Q6: What is CIF?
A: Community Investment Fund routed through federations.

Q7: How is poverty identified?
A: Through Participatory Identification of Poor (PIP).

Q8: Can urban poor apply?
A: No, separate scheme exists for urban poor.
