Stand-Up India

Ministry: Ministry of Finance  
Department: Department of Financial Services  
Launch Date: 5 April 2016  
Type: Bank Loan Facilitation Scheme  

--------------------------------------------------

1. Overview:

Stand-Up India is a scheme by the Ministry of Finance aimed at financing Scheduled Caste (SC), Scheduled Tribe (ST), and Women entrepreneurs by facilitating bank loans for setting up greenfield enterprises.

The scheme supports enterprises in:
- Manufacturing
- Services
- Trading
- Activities allied to agriculture

Objective:
To facilitate bank loans ranging from Rs. 10 lakh to Rs. 1 crore to:
- At least one SC/ST borrower per bank branch
- At least one woman borrower per bank branch

For non-individual enterprises:
At least 51% of the shareholding and controlling stake must be held by an SC/ST or Woman entrepreneur.

Status:
The scheme is currently marked as closed on the myScheme portal.

--------------------------------------------------

2. Benefits:

- Composite loan (term loan + working capital) between Rs. 10 lakh and Rs. 1 crore.
- RuPay debit card issued for borrower convenience.
- Hand-holding support through SIDBI portal, including:
  - Training and skill development
  - Mentoring support
  - Project report preparation
  - Application assistance
  - Work shed / utility support services
  - Access to subsidy schemes

--------------------------------------------------

3. Eligibility:

- Loan is provided only for greenfield enterprises (first-time ventures in manufacturing, services, trading, or allied agricultural activities).
- If applicant is male, he must belong to SC or ST category.
- Women entrepreneurs are eligible.
- Applicant must be at least 18 years old.
- Applicant must not be in default to any bank or financial institution.
- In case of non-individual enterprises, 51% ownership must be held by SC/ST or Woman entrepreneur.

--------------------------------------------------

4. Application Process:

Mode 1:
Approach the nearest bank branch directly.

Mode 2:
Contact the Lead District Manager (LDM) of the district.

Mode 3:
Apply through the official Stand-Up India portal.

Online Portal Process:

Step 1:
Visit the official Stand-Up India portal.

Step 2:
Enter full details of the business location.

Step 3:
Select applicant category:
- SC
- ST
- Woman
Confirm whether 51% stake is held.

Step 4:
Provide business details:
- Nature of proposed business
- Loan amount required
- Description of business
- Details of premises

Step 5:
Enter past business experience details.

Step 6:
Indicate whether hand-holding support is required.

Step 7:
Fill personal details including:
- Name of enterprise
- Constitution of business entity

Step 8:
Submit registration form.

After registration:
The respective financial institution contacts the applicant to complete loan processing and formalities.

--------------------------------------------------

5. Documents Required:

General Documents:

- Proof of Identity (Voter ID / Passport / Driving License / PAN Card)
- Proof of Residence (Utility Bill / Passport / Voter ID)
- Proof of Business Address
- Declaration that applicant is not a defaulter
- Memorandum & Articles of Association (Company) / Partnership Deed
- Assets and liabilities statement of promoters and guarantors
- Latest income tax returns
- Rent agreement (if premises rented)
- Pollution control clearance (if applicable)
- SSI / MSME registration (if applicable)
- Projected balance sheets for next two years
- Title deeds / lease deeds of properties offered as security
- Caste certificate (for SC/ST applicants)
- Certificate of incorporation showing majority stake held by SC/ST/Woman

For Loans Above Rs. 25 Lakh:

- Profile of business unit
- Last three years balance sheets of associate/group companies (if any)
- Detailed project report including:
  - Machinery details
  - Supplier details
  - Financial projections
  - Capacity utilization
  - Profit and loss projections
  - Labour and staffing plans
  - Raw material sources
  - Buyer details
  - Competitor analysis
  - Strength and weakness analysis

--------------------------------------------------

6. Frequently Asked Questions (Selected):

Q: What is the size of the loan under Stand-Up India?
A: Loans range from Rs. 10 lakh to Rs. 1 crore.

Q: What is hand-holding support?
A: Hand-holding support includes assistance in training, skill development, mentoring, project preparation, and loan application facilitation.

Q: Who are eligible lending institutions?
A: Scheduled Commercial Banks and other participating financial institutions under the scheme.

Q: What activities are eligible?
A: Greenfield enterprises in manufacturing, services, trading, and activities allied to agriculture.

Q: What is the difference between Stand-Up India and Start-Up India?
A: Stand-Up India provides bank loans specifically for SC/ST and Women entrepreneurs for greenfield enterprises, whereas Start-Up India focuses on innovation-driven startups and ecosystem development.
