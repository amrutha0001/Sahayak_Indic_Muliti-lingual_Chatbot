Startup India

Ministry: Ministry of Commerce & Industry  
Department: Department for Promotion of Industry and Internal Trade (DPIIT)  
Launch Date: 16 January 2016  
Type: Entrepreneurship Promotion Initiative  

--------------------------------------------------

1. Overview:

Startup India is a flagship initiative aimed at promoting innovation, entrepreneurship, and startup ecosystem growth in India.

Objectives:

• Simplify startup registration process.
• Provide tax incentives.
• Facilitate funding access.
• Promote research and innovation.
• Encourage job creation.

The scheme provides DPIIT recognition to eligible startups, enabling them to access government benefits.

--------------------------------------------------

2. Benefits:

1. DPIIT Recognition.
2. Income tax exemption for 3 consecutive years (eligible startups).
3. Exemption under Section 56 (Angel Tax).
4. Fund of Funds for Startups (FFS).
5. Easier compliance under labor and environmental laws.
6. Fast-track patent application and IPR support.
7. Self-certification compliance.

--------------------------------------------------

3. Eligibility:

• Entity must be incorporated as:
   - Private Limited Company
   - Registered Partnership Firm
   - LLP

• Age of entity must be less than 10 years.
• Annual turnover must not exceed ₹100 crore.
• Must work toward innovation, development, or improvement of products/services.

Exclusions:

• Entities formed by splitting or reconstruction of existing business.
• Large enterprises exceeding turnover cap.

--------------------------------------------------

4. Application Process:

Step 1:
Register entity under Companies Act/LLP Act.

Step 2:
Visit Startup India portal.

Step 3:
Create profile and upload documents.

Step 4:
Provide details of innovation/business model.

Step 5:
Submit for DPIIT recognition.

Step 6:
Receive recognition certificate upon approval.

--------------------------------------------------

5. Required Documents:

• Certificate of Incorporation
• PAN
• Details of Directors/Partners
• Pitch deck/business description
• Authorization letter

--------------------------------------------------

6. Frequently Asked Questions:

Q1: Is DPIIT recognition mandatory?
A: Required to access Startup India benefits.

Q2: Is tax exemption automatic?
A: No, requires separate application.

Q3: Can a sole proprietorship apply?
A: No.

Q4: What is Fund of Funds?
A: Government-backed capital support mechanism.

Q5: How long is recognition valid?
A: Up to 10 years from incorporation.
