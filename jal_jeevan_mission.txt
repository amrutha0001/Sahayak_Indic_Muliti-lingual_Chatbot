Jal Jeevan Mission (JJM)

Ministry: Ministry of Jal Shakti  
Department: Department of Drinking Water & Sanitation  
Launch Date: 15 August 2019  
Type: Centrally Sponsored Scheme  

--------------------------------------------------

1. Overview:

Jal Jeevan Mission (JJM) is a centrally sponsored scheme launched to provide Functional Household Tap Connection (FHTC) to every rural household in India.

The mission ensures the provision of:

• Safe and adequate drinking water  
• At least 55 litres per capita per day (LPCD)  
• Regular and long-term water supply  
• Water quality compliance with BIS standards  

The scheme emphasizes:

• Source sustainability (groundwater recharge, rainwater harvesting)
• Community participation
• Decentralized implementation
• Women-led water governance
• Water quality surveillance

Village-level planning is done through a Village Action Plan (VAP), which includes:

• Water source identification
• Infrastructure planning
• Greywater management
• Operation & maintenance strategy

Funding Pattern:

• 90:10 (Centre:State) for Himalayan and North-Eastern States  
• 50:50 for other States  
• 100% central funding for UTs without legislature  

--------------------------------------------------

2. Benefits:

1. Functional Household Tap Connection (FHTC):
   - Tap connection to every rural household.
   - In-house potable water supply.

2. 55 LPCD Supply:
   - Ensures minimum drinking water quantity.

3. Water Quality Monitoring:
   - District water testing laboratories.
   - Field test kits distributed to villages.
   - Women trained for water quality testing.

4. Infrastructure Development:
   - Piped water supply networks.
   - Storage tanks.
   - Pumping systems.

5. Greywater Management:
   - Soak pits.
   - Drainage channels.
   - Community wastewater systems.

6. Employment Generation:
   - Local workforce participation.
   - Village-level water committees.

7. Operation & Maintenance:
   - Managed by Gram Panchayat/Village Water Committee.
   - Community-based cost recovery model.

--------------------------------------------------

3. Eligibility:

Eligible:

• All rural households without tap water connection.
• Households in water-stressed or quality-affected areas.
• SC/ST dominant habitations prioritized.
• Aspirational districts prioritized.

Not Eligible:

• Urban households.
• Households already covered under piped supply.
• Commercial establishments.

Special Provisions:

• Schools and Anganwadis prioritized.
• Health centers included.
• Water quality-affected villages prioritized (fluoride, arsenic, iron contamination).

--------------------------------------------------

4. Application Process:

Mode: Implemented through State & Gram Panchayat

Step 1:
Village identified under JJM baseline survey.

Step 2:
Preparation of Village Action Plan (VAP).

Step 3:
Approval by District Water & Sanitation Mission.

Step 4:
Fund release to State/Implementing Agency.

Step 5:
Infrastructure construction.

Step 6:
Tap installation in households.

Step 7:
Community-based O&M mechanism established.

Note:
Individual households do not apply directly.
Coverage is through village-level inclusion.

--------------------------------------------------

5. Documents Required:

• Aadhaar (if required locally)
• Household verification by Gram Panchayat
• Address proof (ration card/voter ID)
• Bank details (if user contribution applicable)

--------------------------------------------------

6. Frequently Asked Questions:

Q1: How much water is guaranteed?
A: 55 litres per capita per day.

Q2: Is water completely free?
A: Capital cost is government-funded; user charges may apply for maintenance.

Q3: Who manages maintenance?
A: Gram Panchayat or Village Water & Sanitation Committee.

Q4: What if water source dries up?
A: Source sustainability plans are integrated in VAP.

Q5: Are urban households covered?
A: No.

Q6: How is water quality ensured?
A: Through certified labs and field testing kits.

Q7: What happens in water contamination cases?
A: Immediate remedial measures and alternative supply.

Q8: Is Aadhaar mandatory?
A: Not mandatory centrally; may be required locally.
